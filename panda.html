Pandas are known be two common names, the giant panda (Ailuropoda melanoleuca) the red panda (Ailurus fulgens). However these names are misleading because the two species are not related. The giant panda is a member of the bear family, and the red panda belongs to the racoon family. The giant pandas can be found in the mountainious forested lands in China's Shaanzi, Sichuan, and Gansu provinces. Giant pandas eat nothing but bamboo, and they consume 20 to 45 pounds of bamboo shoots a day to absorb enouth nutrients. The panda's natural habitat has shrunk dramatically over time and are now considered a highl endanged species. Red pandas are often hunted for eventhough it is illegal. They are hunted for their skins which are used to make hats and pelts and are often found in markets through South Asia. Red pandas also eat bamboo along with blossoms, fruit, leaves, eggs and berries. They weigh about 14 pounds and can be about 14 inches in length. They use their long, bushy tails to help maintain balance when climbing trees. Bothe species share a thumb-like growth on their front limbs that helps them grip bamboo. Red pandas and giant pandas inhabit some of the same habitats in some locations.
